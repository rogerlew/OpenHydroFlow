About OpenHydroFlow

Overview

OpenHydroFlow is an open-source web application for water distribution network modeling using EPANET principles. It provides a user-friendly interface for creating, analyzing, and visualizing water distribution systems.

Features
  • Automated data collection from open-source APIs
  • Integration with Madison, WI water infrastructure data
  • Advanced hydraulic modeling based on EPANET principles
  • Interactive network visualization
  • Time series analysis of simulation results
  • Scenario comparison and analysis

Data Sources

OpenHydroFlow uses the following open data sources:

  • City of Madison, WI Open Data Portal
  • USGS National Water Information System
  • EPA SDWIS (Safe Drinking Water Information System)
  • USGS National Map API (for elevation data)
How It Works

OpenHydroFlow follows a four-step workflow:

  1. Data Collection: Gathers water infrastructure GIS data, elevation data, water quality data, and monitoring station data from various APIs.
  2. Data Processing: Cleans and transforms the collected data into a format suitable for hydraulic modeling, including extracting pipe properties, junction elevations, and network connectivity.
  3. Network Modeling: Creates a water distribution model with all necessary components including pipes, junctions, reservoirs, and tanks.
  4. Simulation: Runs hydraulic simulations to calculate pressure, flow, velocity, and other parameters throughout the network over time, generating comprehensive results for analysis and visualization.
Hydraulic Modeling Approach

OpenHydroFlow provides two methods for hydraulic simulation:

  1. EPANET Command-line Tool: The application automatically downloads and configures the EPANET command-line tool for accurate hydraulic simulations.
  2. Built-in Simulator: A simplified hydraulic calculator is included as a fallback when the EPANET command-line tool is not available.

Hydraulic Modeling Principles

EPANET Framework

OpenHydroFlow is based on the principles developed in EPANET, a public domain water distribution system modeling software package. It performs extended-period simulation of hydraulic behavior within pressurized pipe networks.

Key Hydraulic Parameters
  • Pressure: Force exerted by water per unit area, measured in meters or PSI.
  • Flow: Volume of water moving through pipes per unit time, measured in cubic meters per second (mÂ³/s).
  • Velocity: Speed of water flowing through pipes, measured in meters per second (m/s).
  • Head: Energy per unit weight of water, combining elevation and pressure terms.
  • Headloss: Energy lost due to friction as water flows through pipes.
Network Components
Nodes
  • Junctions: Connection points between pipes where water enters or leaves the network.
  • Reservoirs: Infinite external sources of water with constant head.
  • Tanks: Storage facilities with variable volume over time.
Links
  • Pipes: Transport water between nodes with headloss due to friction.
  • Pumps: Add energy to the water to increase head.
  • Valves: Control flow or pressure in the network.
Simulation Principles

The hydraulic simulation in OpenHydroFlow is based on the following principles:

  • Mass Conservation: The flow into a junction equals the flow out of that junction.
  • Energy Conservation: The total energy (head) loss in any loop must equal zero.
  • Headloss Calculation: Uses the Hazen-Williams formula to calculate headloss due to friction in pipes.
  • Extended Period Simulation: Models changes in the system over time, based on varying demands and control settings.
